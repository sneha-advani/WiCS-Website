Programs

FemmeHacks

FemmeHacks is Philly’s first all-women’s collegiate hackathon. FemmeHacks aims to create a welcoming environment for all kinds of hackers–whether at a beginner level or very experienced–and have fun while doing it. FemmeHacks occurs in February of every year. For more information, please visit the FemmeHacks site here.

Side Projects

WiCS Side Projects is a program designed to help women in Computer Science work on CS-related projects outside the classroom. We provide technical advice and resources to help students complete their projects effectively. Participants learn about web development in HTML and CSS, choose an idea for a project they are passionate about, and work with older Penn students and mentors from tech companies to make their vision a reality.

Mentorship

The WiCS mentorship program, also known as the CSterhood, matches freshman and sophomore women interested in computer science with older women studying similar fields. The mentor helps the mentee with class selection, provides career and academic advice, and generally becomes a much-needed, loving guide to achieving success as a woman in CS. We also have mentorship events throughout the year for all of the mentors and mentees to meet up and get to know each other.

WiCS Wellness

To combat the stressful environment we often find ourselves in as engineers, we recently started the WiCS Wellness initiative to promote healthy habits and stress relief for women at Penn. During midterm season, we distribute goodie bags for students to send to each other, filled with baked goods, candy, and positive messages. We hope to create a fun and welcoming environment for all women in computer science at Penn!

Residential Program

The WiCS residential program is dedicated to promoting a supportive learning community for women interested in computer science. Women interested in computer science may apply to live in this residential program. Located in the Kings Court English College House at the University of Pennsylvania, we host various workshops, field trips, outreach programs, dinner discussions, and socials.
